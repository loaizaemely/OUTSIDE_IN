APPENDICULAR SKELETON

They form the lower and upper limbs. They are the rest of the bones belonging to the parts attached to the midline (appendages); specifically, the pairs of extremities and their respective waists (shoulder and hip), are the ones that make the greatest movements.

FUNCTIONS

The appendicular skeleton has motor and locomotor functions, as part of the human skeleton, it fulfills important functions of support, movement, storage of minerals and production of blood cells.
The bones of the appendicular skeleton are the main attachment sites for the muscles. This skeleton includes:

- The bones of the shoulder girdle, the upper limbs (such as arm, forearm, wrist and hand).
- The pelvic girdle and the lower limbs such as (hip, thigh, leg and foot).
