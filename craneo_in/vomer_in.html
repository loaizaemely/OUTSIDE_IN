VOMER

It is a very flat and thin bone; It is trapezoidal in shape and is located vertically in the center of the nasal cavity separating and creating the division between the two symmetrical parts of the nasal cavity.
It forms the inferior-posterior aspect of the septum between the perpendicular plate of the ethmoid bone anterosuperiorly and the palatine bone posteroinferiorly. In addition, the maxilla is linked to the anterior and inferior vomer, the posterior sphenoid bone, and the anterior nasal cartilage.

FUNCTIONS

° It forms the nasal septum together with the perpendicular plate of the ethmoid bone and the cartilage of the nasal septum.
° Provides grooves for the passage of vascular and nervous structures of the nasal cavity.
° Protects the soft tissues of the upper respiratory tract.
° It is in charge of fragmenting the nostrils in two.
° It serves to filter, heat and humidify the air we inspire.

ANATOMY

* Presents superior and horizontal extensions known as the wings of the vomer.
*A perpendicular sheet arranged vertically.
* It has two faces and each has a prominent groove called the nasopalatine groove, for the passage of the nasopalatine nerve and vessels.

HIGHER

The upper edge is divided by two sheets known as wings of the vomer that limit the nostrils at the top, the wings of the vomer articulate with the crest located on the underside of the sphenoid through the channel that separates them.

LOWER

The lower border of the vomer articulates with the nasal crest and with the upper area of the incisive crest at the base of the nostrils; Same with the top edge.

PREVIOUS

The anterior border is divided into two plates, it articulates with the posterior border of the perpendicular plate of the ethmoid and in the inferior part with the cartilage of the nasal septum.

LATER

The posterior edge is responsible for separating the posterior holes of the nostrils.
