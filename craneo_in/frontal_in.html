FRONTAL BONE

The frontal bone of the skull forms the smooth curve of the forehead and protects important structures such as the frontal lobe of the brain and the horizontal plate of the ethmoid bone known as the cribriform plate that gives way through its perforated surface to the olfactory nerve bundles, making possible sense of smell. The frontal bone is part of three regions of the head: the forehead, the orbit, and the nose.
As the frontal bone is the most anterior bone of the skull, it is related both to the adjacent bones of the neurocranium and to the most superior bones of the viscerocranium.

FUNCTIONS

Throughout life, it plays a vital role in protecting important neural structures and forms the superior aspect of the orbit. Fractures of the frontal bone occur in approximately 5% to 15% of all facial fractures.
Its function is based on protecting both the brain and the frontal lobes.
Muscle inserts.
Occipitofrontalis muscle.

ANATOMY

The frontal bone is one of the skull bones that surround the brain (neurocranium) and consists of three parts:

°Flake.      °Orbital portion.      °Nasal portion.     

SCALE

The largest area of the frontal bone is the scale that comprises the forehead area. This contains the frontal sinuses which are located superiorly medial to the orbit and are separated by a septum.
The upper rim of the orbit (supraorbital rim) contains the supraorbital notch through which the vessels and supraorbital nerve pass. The arches above these are known as the superciliary arches (Latin “cilia” = eyelashes). Above the root of the nose is a smooth, slightly raised face known as the glabella.

ORBITAL PORTION

The orbital portion forms the roof of the orbit laterally and medially, around the ethmoid notch, hemiceldillas can be found that will help to form the ethmoid sinuses. The trochlear spine serves as the attachment site for the superior oblique muscle at the medial angle of the eye.
The ethmoidal portion comprises two foramina: the anterior ethmoidal foramen (for the anterior ethmoidal nerve and vessels) and the posterior ethmoidal foramen (for the posterior ethmoidal nerve and vessels).

NASAL PORTION

The nasal spine is formed by the union of the nasal portion of the frontal bone with the frontal processes of the maxillary bone and the nasal bones.
