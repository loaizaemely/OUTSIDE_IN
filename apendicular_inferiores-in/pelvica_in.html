PELVIC GIRDLE

Formed by the coxal or iliac bones; It is a ring of bones attached to the vertebral column that connects the bones of the lower limbs with the axial skeleton.
The pelvic girdle consists of the right and left hip bones. Each hip bone is a large, flattened, irregularly shaped fusion of three bones: the ILIUM, the ISCHIUM, and the PUBIS.

PRINCIPAL FUNCTION

° The pelvis as a whole is capable of performing different movements thanks to the tilting that occurs on the coxofemoral joints (hips) and the joint play that the lumbosacral joint allows.

° Support the weight in the upper part of the body when standing or sitting.

Topographically, the pelvis is divided into two regions, the greater pelvis (also called the false pelvis) and the lesser pelvis (true pelvis). But there are 4 types of pelvis: gynecoid, anthropoid, android and platypeloid.

MAJOR PELVIS

It is the portion of the pelvis located above the superior strait, therefore, of the lesser pelvis or true pelvis. And it is usually considered part of the abdomen. It contains the lower portions of the abdominal organs.

MINOR PELVIS

It is the space enclosed by the pelvic girdle and below the brim of the pelvis: between the pelvic inlet and the pelvic floor. This cavity is a short curved channel, deeper in its posterior part than in its anterior wall.

GYNECOID

It is the type of pelvis that is considered most suitable for labor. It is characterized by having an anteroposterior diameter of dimensions similar to the transverse, sacrum without rectifications, straight lateral walls, spines sciatica slightly prominent and a subpubic angle greater than 90°.

ANTHROPOID

It is also a transversely narrow pelvis. In its upper strait, the anteroposterior diameter is greater than the transverse one. The sciatic notches are large and the sacrum narrow, resulting in a long pelvis.

ANDROID

It is a pelvis reminiscent of the male pelvis. All diameters are insufficient. It is a transversely narrow pelvis, the maximum transverse diameter is far behind, very close to the promontory, so it is not usable for it to fit.

PLATYPELOID

The sacrum is usually flat and straight, of normal length and slightly inclined forwards, so that the antero-posterior diameter of the superior strait is somewhat shorter than the inferior strait. The sub pubic angle is obtuse. It is characterized by its oval shape with a transverse predominance.

COXAL OR ILIAC BONE

The iliac or also called coxal bone, is a bony structure that is part of the hip. Its characteristic is that it is a large element, deflected on its axis and it is also flat. This structure is made up of different zones; the first is the acetabular cavity, followed by the ilium, and finally the obturator foramen, formed anteriorly by the pubis and posteriorly by the ischium.

MEANINGS

Transverse: deepest abdominal muscle. It originates in the lumbar vertebrae and its fibers continue anteriorly through the iliac crest towards the abdominal area, as if it were a belt.

 sciatica: Pain that spreads along the sciatic nerve, from the lower back to one or both legs.
