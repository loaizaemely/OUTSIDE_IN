LOWER EXTREMITIES

The lower limbs and pelvic girdle consist of 2 hip or iliac bones, 2 femurs, 2 patellas, 2 tibias, 2 fibulas, and the foot bones.
The lower limbs include the thigh, leg, and foot bones.
The femur is the only bone in the thigh. It articulates with the two bones of the leg: the larger, called the tibia (commonly known as the "shin"), and the smaller, called the fibula.
The thigh and leg bones articulate based on the knee, which is protected and contained by the patella, which supports the quadriceps tendon.
The bones of the foot include the tarsus, metatarsus, and phalanges.

HOW IS IT DIVIDED?

The lower appendicular skeleton is divided into: thigh with 1 bone; leg with 2; tarsus with 8; metatarsus with 5 and phalanges with 14 bones. Which are named after:

° Pelvic girdle     ° Femur (thigh)      ° Patella (knee)      ° Ankle     

° Tibia and fibula (leg)     ° Tarsus, metatarsus and phalanges (foot)    

° Calcaneus    ° Astragalus    ° Cuboid    ° Navicular    ° Cuneiform
