FEMUR

The femur is the strongest and longest bone in the body, it is located in the thigh, between the knee joint and the pelvis (hip). It is protected by many muscles and serves as a stabilizer for the lower limb.

ANATOMY

The anatomy of the femur is so unique that it makes the bone suitable for supporting numerous muscular and ligamentous attachments in this region, as well as allowing maximum extension of the limb during ambulation.
The femur articulates with the coxal bone. Distally, with the patella and with the proximal aspect of the tibia.

FUNCTIONS

The main function of the femur is to transmit the forces of the tibia in the hip joint. It acts as the origin of many muscles and ligaments.

HOW IS IT DIVIDED?

The femur is divided into:

°Proximal End      °Body      °Distal end     
°Coxofemoral      °Metaphysis      °Knee

PROXIMAL END

Where the head of the femur is located, a spheroid-shaped structure covered with cartilage; in it there is a depression called the fossa of the head of the femur that houses the ligament of the head of the femur.

GREATER TROCHANTER

It is roughly quadrangular and extends from the superior aspect of the junction of the neck and body of the femur. It is associated with a rough depression , crescent-shaped known as the trochanteric fossa, found on the medial aspect of the apophysis .

LESSER TROCHANTER

The lesser trochanter is a bony prominence located between the diaphysis and the neck of the femur. It serves as an insertion for the iliopsoas muscle. The lesser trochanter is attached to the greater trochanter through the intertrochanteric line of the femur.

CREST
INTERTROCHANTERIC

It is a pronounced ridge on the posterior aspect of the femur, beginning at the intersection of the body and neck of the femur. It extends inferomedially from the greater trochanter to the lesser trochanter.

BODY

The body of the femur is a cylindrical structure that varies significantly from one individual to another. Considering in it three faces and three edges: Front, outer and inner faces and inner, outer and upper edges.

FACES

FRONT FACE: smooth and more convex than flat, it is covered, in its upper three quarters, by the crural muscle or deep part of the quadriceps, to which it attaches. Below the crural muscle, this face also gives insertion to the subcrural or tensor synovial muscle of the knee.

EXTERNAL AND INTERNAL FACE: Its convex external face presents the crest of the gluteus medius, where this muscle is inserted. The inner face, on the other hand, is dug vertically. It presents a deep depression, the digital cavity of the greater trochanter, where the external obturator, internal obturator and gemini muscles are inserted.

BORDERS

SUPERIOR BORDER: The upper border, almost horizontal, lends insertion to the pyramidal muscle. Some fascicles of the quadriceps femoris -vastus lateralis- start from the lower border. The quadratus cruris is inserted on the posterior border.

EXTERNAL AND INTERNAL BORDER: Its external, convex face presents the crest of the gluteus medius, where this muscle is inserted. The inner face, on the other hand, is dug vertically. It presents a deep depression, the digital cavity of the greater trochanter, where the external obturator, internal obturator and gemini muscles are inserted..

DISTAL END

The distal femur is not only the widest portion of the bone, but is also related to the proximal tibia and patella. The distal end of the femur is formed by the medial and lateral condyles, the intercondylar fossa, and the patellar aspect.

MEANINGS

Rough Depression: A depression or cavity within or on top of the bone. Large spherical/round shaped process, usually with a rough surface.

 Apophysis: Protruding part of a bone, which serves for its articulation or for muscle insertions

Diaphysis: is defined as that part of the long type bones, which is located in the middle and generally has a prominent and triangular shape. The long bones are those located in the arms, legs, distal part of the hands and feet, and one that belongs to the thorax. This region, unlike most epiphyses, has an outer layer known as the periosteum.

 inferior medially: the term is used to describe general positions of body parts.

 Fascicles: Each one of the parts of a book that are published periodically, generally in the form of a printed notebook.
